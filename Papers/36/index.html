Lifelong MAPF for Shipment Preparation in Stochastic Warehouses

Authors: A. Maoudj, S. Mekid & A. L. Christensen

Published:  |  Journal: 2024 IEEE 20th International Conference on Automation Science and Engineering (CASE), , pp. 10

Abstract

The Multi-Agent Path Finding (MAPF) problem focuses on deriving collision-free paths for multiple agents in a shared environment. However, the traditional formulation of the MAPF problem does not incorporate critical aspects and requirements of shipment preparation tasks, which are essential for efficiency in modern warehouse operations. In this context, we introduce a specialized version of the MAPF problem, termed Lifelong Multi-Agent Path Finding for Shipment Preparation (lifelong MAPF-SP).

Unlike the traditional MAPF paradigm, the lifelong MAPF-SP formulation includes unique features tailored to shipment preparation: (i) each robot must perform several pickup and delivery tasks until the shipment is ready, (ii) both pickup actions and delivery actions have unknown stochastic durations, and (iii) all deliveries must be made to a designated shipment loading area.
